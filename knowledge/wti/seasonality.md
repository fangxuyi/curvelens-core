# WTI Seasonality — How to Read the Same Number in Different Months

*A 4 MMbbl June crude draw is seasonal; the same draw in January is a signal.
Judge every weekly print against the season, not just the raw WoW change.*

## The refined-products year

| Window | What happens | How it reads in the data |
|---|---|---|
| **Feb–Apr** | Spring refinery turnarounds | crude BUILDS are normal (runs down), product draws; low utilization (~85%) is maintenance, not weakness |
| **May–Aug** | Driving season (Memorial Day → Labor Day) | crude DRAWS are normal; gasoline draws expected; utilization runs hot (93–97%) |
| **Jun–Nov** | Atlantic hurricane season (peak Aug–Sep) | Gulf shut-ins → sudden supply draws + refinery outages → crude builds with product draws; distinguish disruption from trend |
| **Sep–Oct** | Fall turnarounds | crude builds normal again; gasoline specs switch (winter RVP) |
| **Nov–Feb** | Heating season | distillate draws matter most; cold snaps move HO first, crude second |

## Practical rules for the brief

- **Crude draw in Feb–Apr or Sep–Oct** → more bullish than it looks (draws
  against the turnaround grain).
- **Crude build in Jul** → more bearish than it looks (builds against the
  driving-season grain).
- **Refinery utilization** should be read against its seasonal band: 88% in
  March is normal; 88% in July is demand weakness or unplanned outages.
- **Gasoline vs distillate divergence** flags which demand engine is running:
  driving-season gasoline draws with distillate builds = consumer strong /
  industrial soft (the 2026-07-02 report showed exactly this pattern).
- Hurricane headlines require separating *supply* impact (Gulf production
  shut-ins, bullish crude) from *demand* impact (refinery shutdowns, bearish
  crude / bullish products) — they often arrive together.
