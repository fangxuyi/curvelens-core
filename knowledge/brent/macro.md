# Brent Macro and Cross-Market Analysis

*Last reviewed: 2026-07-23 — provenance: verified source definitions plus authored interpretation priors*

The profile uses FRED for dated Brent and WTI spot cross-checks, the broad
dollar, U.S. industrial production, and short-term Treasury yield. It also
collects a delayed WTI continuous-futures benchmark for approximate regional
spread context.

These are context inputs, not replacements for authoritative ICE settlements.
Never mix unsynchronized closes into a tradable Brent-WTI spread claim. Report
source, date, and timing, and distinguish:

- changes in the global oil balance;
- regional dislocations and arbitrage;
- currency and broad risk sentiment;
- financing/carry effects;
- geopolitical risk premia.

Compare macro and physical explanations with the Brent curve and option
surface. A stronger dollar or weaker industrial indicator can coexist with a
rising Brent price when physical tightness or geopolitical risk dominates.
