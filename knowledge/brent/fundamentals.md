# Brent Physical Fundamentals

*Last reviewed: 2026-07-23 — provenance: verified official sources plus authored collection policy*

Brent is a global crude benchmark, so its physical analysis cannot be reduced
to one inventory series. Keep these channels separate:

- BFOET/North Sea production, maintenance, loading programs, quality, and
  benchmark methodology;
- OPEC+ production policy, compliance, spare capacity, and export behavior;
- Atlantic Basin imports/exports, arbitrage economics, tanker availability,
  freight, sanctions, and voyage disruption;
- refinery runs, margins, outages, crude quality demand, and product cracks;
- OECD and non-OECD demand, strategic stocks, commercial inventories, and
  floating storage.

The deterministic EIA weekly feed provides timely U.S. crude, Cushing, product,
and refinery context. It is relevant to Atlantic balances and Brent-WTI
arbitrage, but it is not a direct Brent inventory report. Use the
[EIA Weekly Petroleum Status Report](https://www.eia.gov/petroleum/supply/weekly/)
with its release date and covered week. Use dated IEA, OPEC, EIA outlook,
North Sea operator, customs, port, tanker, and sanctions evidence according to
their actual geography and cadence.

Do not treat an announced OPEC+ target as observed production, a loading
program as completed exports, or a tanker diversion as lost supply without
checking duration and destination.
