# Brent Regimes and Reference Hierarchy

*Last reviewed: 2026-07-23 — provenance: uncalibrated interpretation priors*

Brent starts without enough local ICE history for stable regime bands.

1. Use exact authoritative settlements and measured `history_context`, stating
   the observation count.
2. Treat flat price, calendar spreads, Brent-WTI context, positioning,
   volatility, skew, and wings separately.
3. Use physical evidence with its geography and covered period.
4. Separate a persistent balance change from a temporary outage, freight
   shock, loading delay, or geopolitical premium.

Backwardation can indicate prompt scarcity or strong convenience yield, but it
does not alone identify the source. Contango can reflect surplus, storage
economics, weak prompt demand, or financing. Recalibrate only from measured
Brent history; do not import WTI percentile bands.
