# Copper Macro Analysis

*Last reviewed: 2026-07-23 — provenance: verified source definitions plus authored interpretation priors*

The profile collects official FRED observations for the broad U.S. dollar,
global copper price, U.S. industrial production, manufacturing output, housing
starts, and durable-goods orders. Every observation keeps its date and cadence.

Use the broad dollar as a translation and global-financial-conditions input,
not a complete Copper model. Industrial production, manufacturing, housing,
and durable-goods orders are lagged U.S. demand proxies; none measures
same-day global consumption or China. The monthly global copper-price series is
a cross-check, not an independent fundamental driver of daily HG.

Daily interpretation should compare:

- HG settlement, curve spreads, and CFTC positioning;
- HX ATM volatility, skew, wings, and validated probability surfaces;
- newly released industrial data versus prior values and expectations;
- dated China, mine, smelter, refined-stock, scrap, trade, grid, transport, and
  construction evidence.

State whether macro, physical, and market evidence confirm or conflict. A
coincident price move and news headline is not proof of causation.
