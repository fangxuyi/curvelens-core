# Copper Regimes and Reference Hierarchy

*Last reviewed: 2026-07-23 — provenance: uncalibrated interpretation priors*

Copper starts without enough local HG/HX history for stable numerical regime
bands. Use exact values and this hierarchy:

1. Prefer measured `history_context`, always stating its observation count.
2. Treat flat price, curve, positioning, ATM volatility, risk reversal, and
   butterfly as separate dimensions.
3. Compare physical evidence at the stage it actually measures: concentrate,
   refined metal, scrap, trade, inventory, or fabrication.
4. Preserve geography and cadence. U.S. monthly production is not current
   Chinese demand, and one exchange's warehouse decline is not global stocks.

Contango may reflect financing, storage, abundant nearby metal, delivery
economics, or liquidity. Backwardation may indicate nearby tightness, but the
curve alone does not prove a physical shortage. Recalibrate this file only
after the deployment has a meaningful measured history.
